    >>55944
    That's pretty much exactly it. You have to position them in space (basically they're just points, the bone is just a display convenience), hierarchise them, them apply their influence to each of them. Influence of 1 between a bone (or joint) and a vertex means the vertex will undergo 100% of the Translation, Rotation and Scaling the bone does (you should never scale skeletons though).
    That's "skinning", then "rigging" is more setting up advanced controls for the skeleton to break the hierarchy where you need it (like allow to pull the whole body when pulling the hand) or higher-level controls (like creating a "fist" slider which will close the whole hand instead of having to rotate each phalanx individually).
    There's also the term "setup", which I think includes all of skeleton creation, skinning and rigging. This can be one of the most technical parts in CG (thats what "Technical Directors" do), and it can involve scripting, so be warned.
    Though again if you stick to animation you won't probably go that far.
